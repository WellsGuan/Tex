\section{Basic boundedness statement}

Consider the degenerate parabolic equation
\[
    u_t = \frac12 u u_{xx}, \qquad (t,x)\in [0,\infty)\times \R,
\]
with nonnegative bounded initial datum
\[
    0\le u_0(x) \le M := \norm{u_0}_{L^{\infty}(\R)}.
\]

\begin{proposition}
Assume that the solution belongs to a class for which comparison holds. Then for all $t\ge 0$ and $x\in \R$,
\[
    0 \le u(t,x) \le M.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
    \norm{u(t,\cdot)}_{L^{\infty}(\R)} \le \norm{u_0}_{L^{\infty}(\R)}
    \qquad \text{for all } t\ge 0.
\]
\end{proposition}

The reason is that the constant functions
\[
    \underline{u}(t,x)\equiv 0,
    \qquad
    \overline{u}(t,x)\equiv M
\]
are both stationary solutions of the equation, and the initial datum satisfies
\[
    \underline{u}(0,x) \le u_0(x) \le \overline{u}(0,x).
\]
Hence the comparison principle yields the bound.

\begin{remark}
At a formal level, if $u(t,\cdot)$ attains a spatial maximum at a point $x_t$, then
\[
    u_x(t,x_t)=0, \qquad u_{xx}(t,x_t)\le 0,
\]
and therefore
\[
    u_t(t,x_t)=\frac12 u(t,x_t)u_{xx}(t,x_t)\le 0.
\]
This shows that the maximum should not increase in time.
\end{remark}
